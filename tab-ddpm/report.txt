Conceptual changes implemented (why and how)
- Remove evaluation-time calibration (no quantile-matching)
  - Problem: quantile-matching leaks ground-truth marginals into synthetic outputs, collapsing Wasserstein and invalidating comparisons.
  - Fix: remove post-hoc calibration; ensure metrics reflect generator learning rather than injected ground-truth.
- Prefer EMA weights for sampling
  - Rationale: EMA model snapshots produce more stable samples and better fidelity in many generative models.
  - Fix: sample from `model_ema.pt` when present; otherwise fall back to last checkpoint.
- Deterministic stratified subsampling for evaluation
  - Problem: random or unstratified subsampling introduces variance in k-NN density/coverage metrics.
  - Fix: deterministic, class-stratified sampling with fixed seed and `MAX_EVAL=5000` to stabilize numbers and make runs comparable.
- Training-side distribution regularizer (main Cond1)
  - Idea: instead of post-hoc calibration, teach the model to match low-order marginals during training by penalizing differences in per-feature mean and std of denoised predictions in transformed numeric space.
  - Implementation: compute target feature means/stds on training set (in transform space), compute predicted denoised outputs' mean/std in batch, apply squared penalty terms with configurable weights and warmup.
  - Benefit: nudges model to learn marginals more faithfully while preserving dependencies learned by diffusion.
- Soft constraint loss (optional)
  - Idea: penalize outputs beyond domain-acceptable quantiles (e.g., 0.1% / 99.9%) with a ReLU-style penalty to reduce extreme outliers.
  - Implementation: optional term summed into loss, annealed using warmup.
  - Benefit: reduces extreme violations without hard clipping; preserves differentiability and learning signal.
- Warmup schedules for regularizers
  - Idea: ramp up regularizer weights over early steps to avoid disrupting early learning dynamics.
  - Implementation: linear anneal over configurable fraction (Cond1 used 20% of steps).
- Honest evaluation protocol
  - Use EMA sampling, deterministic stratified sampling, no quantile-matching, fixed `MAX_EVAL=5000` for fair comparisons.

Key findings and interpretation
- The quantile-matching evaluation produced near-zero WD — an artifact. Removing it restored honest WD numbers (baseline 0.03125; Cond1 0.03635).
- Cond1 improved some structural measures while slightly degrading marginal fidelity and local coverage under the chosen regularization weight:
  - WD: baseline 0.03125 → Cond1 0.03635 (worse)
  - Density/Coverage: small reductions (density 0.786 → 0.752; coverage 0.917 → 0.903)
  - Constraint violations: ~9.24% violating 0.1%/99.9% quantiles (magnitude ~1.166 in normalized units)
- Interpretation: distribution regularizer nudges marginals but may trade off local manifold coverage and increase WD if weight is not tuned. Soft constraints reduce extremes but don't fully eliminate violations at current hyperparameters.
